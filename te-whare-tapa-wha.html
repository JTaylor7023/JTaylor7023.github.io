My Wellbeing Plan Using Te Whare Tapa Whā

Based on Sir Mason Durie’s model of wellbeing, this plan includes strategies for nurturing all aspects of my health: physical, spiritual, mental, social, and environmental.

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

  • Exercise 3–4 times a week (walking, stretching, strength)
  • Eat fresh, balanced meals and stay hydrated
  • Prioritize sleep and rest

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

  • Practice mindfulness or meditation daily
  • Connect with nature and reflect on purpose
  • Take time for gratitude and self-reflection
  • Engage in cultural or spiritual practices

Taha Whānau (Family & Social Wellbeing)

  • Stay connected with whānau and friends
  • Schedule regular catch-ups or video calls
  • Offer support and ask for help when needed
  • Celebrate achievements and milestones together

Taha Hinengaro (Mental & Emotional Wellbeing)

  • Journal thoughts and feelings weekly
  • Talk to a trusted person or counselor if needed
  • Take breaks and manage screen time
  • Engage in hobbies that spark joy and creativity

Taha Whenua (Land & Environment)

  • Spend time outdoors regularly
  • Care for the local environment
  • Reflect on my connection to the land and place
