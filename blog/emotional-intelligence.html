The Power of Emotional Intelligence

Published on September 8, 2025

In today's hyper-connected, constantly evolving world, emotional intelligence (EI) isn't just a "nice to have" trait, it's a core skill. Whether you're steering a team, nurturing a relationship, or pursuing personal growth, the ability to recognise, understand and manage emotions is pivotal for success and wellbeing.

What Is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is the capacity to perceive, interpret, regulate, and influence emotions, both our own and those of others. Daniel Goleman, a leading voice on the subject, identifies five foundational pillars:

Self-awareness: Knowing what you feel and how those feelings shape your thoughts and actions.
Self-regulation: Steering emotions constructively, especially under pressure.
Motivation: Channeling emotions to pursue goals with vigor and perseverance.
Empathy: Sensing others' emotions and responding with genuine care.
Social skills: Building and maintaining healthy relationships through effective communication and conflict resolution.

Why Does It Matter?

High EI leads to stronger connections, better mental health, superior job performance, and sstronger leadership. Research shows that emotional intelligence can out predict raw IQ when it comes to real world achievement.

"It is very important to understand that emotional intelligence is not the opposite of intelligence, it is not the triumph of heart over head - it is the unique intersection of both." - David Caruso

Can You Boost Your Emotional Intelligence?

Absolutely! like any skill, EI grows with deliberate practice. Try these starter steps:

  • Reflect daily on your emotional reactions and pinpoint triggers.
  • Incorporate mindfulness or meditation to stay present.
  • Ask trusted friends for candid feedback on how you handle emotions.
  • Study role models who demonstrate strong emotional insight.

Final Thoughts

Emotional intelligence isn't about bottling up feelings; it's about understanding them deeply and using that awareness to make your own life and the lives of those around you. In this modern world brimming with complexity and human interaction, investing in EI can pay lifelong dividends.
